# Research Intelligence

Use this umbrella for research-oriented work: finding papers, monitoring expert blogs/RSS, building/querying knowledge bases, querying prediction markets, or writing ML/AI research papers.

## Literature and paper discovery

Use arXiv/search tooling for papers by keyword, author, category, or ID. Capture bibliographic details, links, abstracts, and relevance notes. For paper writing, connect claims to citations and keep venue formatting constraints explicit.

## Research monitoring

Use RSS/blog monitoring when the user wants ongoing awareness of authors, labs, product blogs, or technical feeds. Distinguish one-off search from recurring monitoring.

## Knowledge bases and notes

Use LLM-wiki-style workflows for interlinked markdown research knowledge. Prefer durable source-linked notes over opaque summaries.

## Prediction markets and live signals

Use Polymarket-style APIs for market prices, order books, historical prices, and event probabilities. Report timestamps and never treat market prices as ground truth.

## Paper writing pipeline

For NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR/ACL-style writing, track contribution, experiments, baselines, figures, citations, reviewer expectations, and submission templates. Keep LaTeX/template assets with the package that owns them.

## Verification

Research outputs should cite source URLs/IDs and state retrieval date or API timestamp when live data is involved.
