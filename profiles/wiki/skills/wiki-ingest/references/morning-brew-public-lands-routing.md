# Morning Brew Public Lands / Sunday Special Routing

Session pattern: Morning Brew may send a Sunday special issue that is not a normal market table or business-news roundup. Treat it as a substantive newsletter source when it contains durable public-policy, infrastructure, tourism, safety, conservation, or culture material.

## Example: July 12 2026 “National Parks Brew”

Durable items extracted:
- National Park Service capacity mismatch: budget cuts, staffing reductions, fee-collection gaps, deferred maintenance, crowd management, visitor safety.
- Domestic tourism infrastructure: parks as economic engines for gateway towns, with NPS-reported output/jobs and reliance on federal maintenance capacity.
- Dark-sky parks / stargazing: national parks as managed astronomy-tourism infrastructure, including light-pollution controls and dark-sky programming.

Good routing outcome:
- Create/update a public-lands or infrastructure note when the issue has enough operational detail, e.g. `US National Parks Capacity Strain`.
- Refresh tourism/economic notes when the issue quantifies visitor spending, jobs, gateway-town effects, or travel-capacity constraints.
- Refresh culture/science notes when the issue adds durable context such as dark-sky parks, astronomy tourism, conservation education, or recreation practices.
- Update `Morning Brew Newsletter Network` with one concise routing paragraph so future issues know how to triage the stream.
- Route US public-lands/policy notes through `MOCs/06 US Politics & Society MOC.md`; use `17 Culture MOC` only if the durable theme is primarily culture/recreation rather than policy/infrastructure.

Usually source-only:
- Sponsor blocks, quiz/game material, mailbag anecdotes, referral links, product recommendations, newsletter-directory/footer boilerplate.

Verification checklist:
- Source archive named `YYYY-MM-DD - Source Title.md` and includes `## Pages Updated`.
- New/updated notes cite the normalized source archive, not the random Inbox filename.
- Relevant MOC, `System/wiki-index.md`, and `System/wiki-log.md` have exact searchable links.
- Inbox is empty after moving the capture to `Sources/`.
