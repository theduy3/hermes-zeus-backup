# Anal Fissure Recovery Protocol

**⚠️ Not medical advice.** See a doctor if bleeding persists beyond 2 weeks or pain increases.

## Core Principle
A fissure is a tear that re-opens with every hard bowel movement. The goal is soft, easy-to-pass stool.

## Fiber: 25-30g/Day (Ramp Gradually)

| Week | Target | Strategy |
|------|--------|----------|
| Week 1 | 20g | Soluble fiber only (gentler) |
| Week 2 | 25-28g | Add insoluble sources |
| Week 3+ | 28-30g | Maintain |

### Soluble Fiber Sources (start here)
- Rolled oats (80g = 8g)
- Banana (1 medium = 3g)
- Avocado (1/2 = 5g)
- Psyllium husk (1 tsp in water = 3g)
- Sweet potato (1 medium = 4g)

### Insoluble Fiber (add week 2)
- Broccoli (1 cup = 5g)
- Mixed berries (1 cup = 8g)
- Ground flaxseed (2 tbsp = 4g)
- Whole wheat bread (1 slice = 3g)

## Hydration: 3L Minimum
Fiber without water = concrete. Fiber with water = soft bulk.

## Natural Stool Softeners (Daily)

| Food | Dose | Mechanism |
|------|------|-----------|
| Kiwifruit | 2/day | Actinidin enzyme stimulates bowel motility |
| Prunes | 2-3/day | Sorbitol + fiber = natural osmotic laxative |
| Ground flaxseed | 1-2 tbsp/day | Mucilage coats and softens stool |
| Olive oil | 1 tbsp raw | Lubricates GI tract |
| Magnesium citrate | 200-400mg/day | Osmotic — draws water into colon |
| Prune juice | 125ml (alternative) | Sorbitol + fiber, natural osmotic |
| Bone broth | 1 cup/day | Collagen/gelatin for tissue repair |
| Kefir / plain yogurt | 1 serving/day | Probiotics for gut motility & stool consistency |

## Foods to Limit
- Hard cheese / heavy dairy (constipating)
- White bread/pasta alone (zero fiber)
- Large portions of red meat (slow digestion)
- Green/unripe bananas (binding)
- Alcohol (dehydrating)
- Excessive caffeine (diuretic — dries stools)
- Spicy foods (irritate during passage)
- Raw tough veg, seeds, popcorn (insoluble bulk → strain)

## Magnesium-Rich Foods
- Pumpkin seeds (¼ cup = 190mg)
- Spinach, cooked (½ cup = 80mg)
- Almonds (¼ cup = 100mg)
- Black beans (½ cup = 60mg)

## Sitz Baths
- 10-15 min warm water, 2x/day minimum
- Most important: after bowel movements
- Relaxes sphincter, increases blood flow, promotes healing

## Integration With Nutrition Plan
- Add flaxseed and prunes to brunch oatmeal
- 2 kiwis with Greek yogurt bowl
- Olive oil on dinner vegetables
- Sweet potato instead of white potato
- All fits within 1,950 cal / 150g protein targets
