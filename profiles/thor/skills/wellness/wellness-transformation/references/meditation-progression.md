# Meditation Progression — Breath Awareness

Beginner practice. One technique: breath at the nostrils.

## Setup
- Sit on cushion, chair, or floor — spine upright but not rigid
- Hands on thighs or in lap
- Eyes closed (or soft gaze downward)
- Timer set — then forget it exists
- Phone in another room, not on silent — gone

## The Practice (20 min)

### Phase 1 — Settle (2-3 min)
Just arrive. Feel sit bones. Notice sounds. Don't control anything. Let the day fall away.

### Phase 2 — The Anchor (15 min)
Bring attention to the breath at the nostrils. Feel cool air entering, warm air leaving. That's it.

Mind will wander within 10 seconds. This isn't failure — it's the design. Silently say "thinking" and return to the nostrils. No frustration. No judgment. The return IS the rep.

### Phase 3 — Closing (2 min)
Let breath be natural. Notice how you feel compared to when you sat down. Open eyes slowly.

## Progression: 20 → 60 Minutes

| Phase | Weeks | Duration |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Foundation | 1-2 | 20 min |
| Settling | 3-4 | 25 min |
| Deepening | 5-6 | 30 min |
| Expand | 7-10 | +5 min every 2 weeks (35→40→45) |
| Anchor | 11-14 | +5 min every 2 weeks (50→55) |
| Target | 15-16 | 60 min |

## Rules
- No music, no guided track after week 1. Learn to sit with silence.
- Attend is the only metric. Duration and quality will fluctuate.
- If you miss a day, drop back to 20 min next session. Speed isn't the goal.
- At 30 min, add body scanning or noting. Before 30 min, just nostrils.

## What to Expect (Week 1-2)
- Mind racing, can't focus → everyone starts here
- Falling asleep → sleep-deprived, sit straighter, eyes slightly open
- Itching, fidgeting → don't move for 10 sec, usually fades
- "I'm doing it wrong" → if you're sitting and returning to breath, you're doing it right
- Boredom → good, boredom is the doorway. Keep sitting.
